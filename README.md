## 🤖 AI Sourcing Tools — How I Use Them

I integrate AI tools at every stage of the funnel. Here's my actual workflow:

### HireEZ
I use HireEZ to aggregate profiles across LinkedIn, GitHub, and 40+ other sources in a single search. For LatAm sourcing specifically, I filter by country, English proficiency signals in the profile, and role-specific keywords. It cuts initial sourcing time by roughly 60% compared to manual LinkedIn search.

### Gem
Once I have a sourced list, Gem handles sequenced outreach — a 3-touch cadence across LinkedIn InMail, email, and a follow-up. I personalize the first message manually and let Gem automate the sequence. I track open rates and response rates weekly and adjust messaging based on what converts.

### SeekOut
I use SeekOut for harder-to-fill roles where LinkedIn alone isn't enough — technical profiles, niche skill sets, or diversity-focused searches. Its filters go deeper than standard ATS tools.

### Claude AI & ChatGPT
My daily drivers. I use them to:
- Generate and refine Boolean strings for new role types
- Write personalized outreach messages at scale
- Summarize CVs and compare candidates against a scorecard
- Draft job descriptions that attract LatAm talent for US roles
- Build interview question banks by competency

## 📋 Candidate Evaluation Scorecard

Developed from 20+ years of interviewing across cultures and industries. I use this for all LatAm → US placements regardless of role level.

| Criteria | Weight | What I Look For |
|---|---|---|
| English Communication | 30% | Spontaneous, natural speech — not rehearsed answers. Writing clarity. |
| Ownership Mindset | 25% | Do they take initiative or wait to be told? Past examples of self-directed results. |
| US Work Culture Fit | 20% | Comfort with async communication, directness, accountability without micromanagement. |
| Technical Role Match | 15% | Hard skills aligned to JD. Verified through specific scenario questions. |
| Stability & Reliability | 10% | Tenure patterns, home office setup, internet quality, references. |

**My threshold:** 70%+ overall to move forward. A candidate who scores below 65% on English Communication does not advance regardless of other scores — this has been the #1 predictor of placement failure in my experience.

## ✅ My Go / No-Go Decision Framework

Before I send a candidate to a hiring manager, I run through this mentally:

- Have I seen evidence of results, not just responsibilities, in their career?
- Would they hold their own in a video call with a senior US executive — unscripted?
- Can they work independently without hand-holding across time zones?
- Is their English natural enough that communication won't become a friction point?
- Would I stake my professional reputation on this recommendation?

If I hesitate on any of these, I don't send them. I go back to sourcing.

This discipline is what took time-to-fill at M Group Residential from **60 days to 11.9 days** — not by moving faster, but by getting the front-end evaluation right the first time.

## 📄 My Recruiting SOP — Sourcing to Day 90

**Step 1 — Role Intake**
Meet with the hiring manager. I don't just collect a job description — I ask what the last person in this role did wrong, what an A-player looks like at 6 months, and what the deal-breakers are. This calibration saves weeks.

**Step 2 — Boolean Build**
Build role-specific search strings using the library above. I create 3–4 variations and test response rates in the first 48 hours before committing to a full outreach push.

**Step 3 — Sourcing**
Run HireEZ and LinkedIn Recruiter simultaneously. Target 150–200 profiles per role. Filter down to 40–50 outreach-ready candidates based on profile signals.

**Step 4 — Outreach**
Launch Gem sequence: personalized first touch, 2 follow-ups over 10 days. Target 25–30% response rate. If below 20%, I revise messaging before continuing.

**Step 5 — Screening**
15-minute structured video call using the scorecard above. I score in real time, not from memory.

**Step 6 — Shortlist**
Maximum 3 candidates submitted per role. If I can't find 3 I'm confident in, I don't submit 3 — I go back to sourcing and tell the hiring manager why.

**Step 7 — Client Debrief**
After each client interview, I collect structured feedback: what they liked, what concerned them, what they'd change about the profile. This recalibrates my next sourcing cycle.

**Step 8 — Placement Follow-Up**
Check-in calls at Day 7, Day 30, and Day 90. Early flags get addressed before they become exits.

## 💬 AI Prompts I Use Daily

**Generate a Boolean string:**
> "Generate a LinkedIn Boolean search string for a [role title] based in Latin America, remote, with strong English, for a US company in [industry]. Include 3 variations."

**Write outreach message:**
> "Write a 4-sentence LinkedIn InMail for a [role title] candidate in [country]. The company is a US [industry] firm hiring remotely. Tone: direct, professional, not salesy. Include one specific hook about remote work in USD."

**Summarize and score a CV:**
> "Read this CV and score the candidate against these criteria: English proficiency (signals in profile), leadership experience, remote work history, and LatAm → US placement fit. Give a score out of 10 with reasoning."

**Draft a job description:**
> "Write a job description for a [role] at a US [industry] company hiring remotely in Latin America. Emphasize: English requirement, remote culture, growth opportunity, USD compensation. Tone: direct, no corporate fluff."
